Gastrointestinal tract

The initial part of the GI tract where food is mechanically broken down by teeth and mixed with saliva for easier swallowing.

A muscular tube that transports food from the pharynx to the stomach using peristalsis.

A passage that connects the mouth to the esophagus; aids in swallowing by directing food into the esophagus.

The primary site for digestion and absorption of nutrients.

Produces enzymes and absorbs nutrients; responsible for most chemical digestion.

Responsible for absorbing water and forming stool.

The final part of the GI tract through which waste is expelled from the body.

Produces bile, which aids in fat digestion, and performs detoxification functions.

Stores and releases bile into the duodenum as needed.

Produces enzymes to break down proteins, fats, and carbohydrates and regulates blood sugar levels through insulin.
